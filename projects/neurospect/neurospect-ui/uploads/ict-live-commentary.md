# ICT Live Commentary: Pre-Market Narrative & Trading Protocol

Live tape-reading and trading protocol as demonstrated by MrWitness and AXL in weekly Discord streams. Stream content is lower-level than structured classes — it shows how classroom concepts apply in real-time, session by session and day by day. This page captures the live application framework rather than the theoretical definitions (those live in the other `ict-*.md` pages).

---

## Pre-Market Opening Routine (MrWitness)

When MrWitness joins the stream, he follows a consistent routine before calling anything:

1. **Mark opening prices:** midnight open, 8:30 open, 9:30 open, range settlement (previous RTH close ~4 PM)
2. **Mark weekly 50%** and any visible higher-timeframe FVGs
3. **Identify the DOL:** nearest 1H/15M swing high (BSL) and swing low (SSL) — which side price is gravitating toward
4. **State initial bias explicitly:** "I'm bearish today" / "looking for longs toward X" with one-sentence reason (HTF FVG, pending business on ES, opening-price position)
5. **Give the two-scenario framework:** "If they take X first, I want to see Y. If they don't and instead go Z, that changes the approach entirely."
6. **Declare the expected HOD/LOD:** names the expected AM session extreme in advance — "I want this to be the low of the day. I don't want to see this low taken out anymore."

This routine is done out loud, live, before any setup forms. The expectation is set publicly and defended or abandoned based on what price does next.

## HOD/LOD Calling Process

MrWitness calls the low (or high) of the AM session — often before it prints — using converging factors:

**Primary signals:**
- **Opening price stack:** Price below midnight + 8:30 + 9:30 opens simultaneously → session low is very likely already in; look for longs only
- **Overnight displacement:** If London / pre-market already delivered a large range in one direction, the AM extreme is likely set
- **Pending business on ES:** If ES hasn't yet taken a nearby level (previous day high, previous week high), MrWitness waits before calling a reversal — "ES still has pending business up here"
- **Speed and velocity post-setup:** Once a level is tapped and price starts spooling away quickly, the HOD/LOD call is confirmed

**FOMC/NFP exception:** Never call HOD/LOD on high-impact news day before the release. Let news print, then read price.

**After the call:**
- "This is the low of the day" → every subsequent pullback is a buy opportunity; do not wait for a new lower low
- "I don't want to see this being taken out" → if it gets taken out on higher-than-normal conditions, call it manual intervention
- Once the target is hit: "I'm content with this. I'm done."

## Day-of-Week Trading Protocol

| Day                            | Conditions                             | Approach                                                    |
| ------------------------------ | -------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Monday**                     | Engineering liquidity for Tue/Wed      | Trade carefully; note direction; expect opposite on Tuesday |
| **Monday (no news, CPI week)** | HRLR; low probability                  | Watch only; best setups come Wednesday                      |
| **Tuesday**                    | Highest probability; forms HOD or LOD  | Trade full sessions; all models apply                       |
| **Wednesday AM**               | High probability; done by 11 AM        | Execute through AM; step aside before lunch                 |
| **Wednesday PM**               | Choppy; building uncertainty for news  | Stay out after 11 AM–noon                                   |
| **Thursday (pre-NFP)**         | Very low probability                   | Do not trade; rewatch live streams; backtest                |
| **Friday (NFP day)**           | Volatile; 15–30 min after news release | Only after release; if week already expanded, skip          |
| **Fed Chair Testifies day**    | High resistance; avoid NY PM           | Lunch session is best window; next day also cautious        |
| **FOMC day**                   | Choppy AM; volatile PM                 | AM session only; no positions into PM release               |
| **ISM/PMI at 10 AM**           | Can be the push price needs            | Wait for 10 AM; best entries appear right after             |
| **CPI / Jackson Hole week**    | HRLR all week                          | Trade Mon–Wed AM only; Thu–Fri avoid                        |

> "Know when not to trade more than when to trade." — MrWitness

## Economic Calendar Protocols

### NFP Week
- **Monday:** Trade normally; market engineers liquidity for Tuesday
- **Tuesday:** Trade full day; primary reversal day
- **Wednesday:** Done by 11 AM — "be done before lunch"
- **Thursday:** Avoid entirely; price is choppy and back-and-forth as it approaches Friday
- **Friday (NFP):** 15–30 minutes after the 8:30 release; if the week has already expanded strongly, be content and skip Friday

### FOMC Day (Wednesday PM)
- AM session: trade normally toward morning targets
- Expected behavior: choppy AM until target is hit; then range-bound until 1 PM
- Previous live FOMC example: MrWitness called the exact LOD from a specific HTF FVG before the event using the HTF bias
- Do NOT take positions ahead of the 1 PM release

### Fed Chair Testifies (Powell, usually Tuesday)
- Same-day AM session: follow normal routine; don't force setups
- **Best window:** lunch session (11:30 AM – 1:30 PM)
- Avoid NY PM day-of and the following day
- "You don't want to be trading the rest of the day" once morning targets are hit on these days

### ISM / PMI (10 AM) or JOLTS (10 AM)
- Morning session: trade carefully; wait for 10 AM
- News at 10 AM often provides the "push" to fill a pending FVG or take an important liquidity level
- After the news print: cleanest entry conditions for the day typically appear

### CPI Week Monday (no-news Monday)
- "A.M. session without news on a Monday before CPI week — that's when you wait."
- Market is engineering liquidity for Wednesday's CPI release
- Mark the levels, note direction, do not trade meaningfully
- Any move Monday makes will be reversed Tuesday/Wednesday

## Weekly Directional Framework

### Monday's Role in Liquidity Engineering

Monday's price action telegraphs Tuesday/Wednesday's delivery direction.

**Pattern:**
- **Monday delivers higher (bullish):** Tuesday/Wednesday will take Monday's lows (SSL) and then rally; Monday engineered the sell-side liquidity that Tuesday sweeps before continuing higher — or forms the HIGH of the week
- **Monday delivers lower (bearish):** Only buy-side targets remain for the week; Tuesday/Wednesday take Monday's lows and rally hard; "the paradigm has changed — we're seeking buy-side only now"

**The tell (YouTube: +1000 Points NQ, March 2026):**
- If Monday does NOT reverse from the sell-side liquidity pool MrWitness was watching, the weekly approach switches
- Monday delivering lower while bearish = no sell-side left; market must deliver to buy-side Tuesday/Wednesday
- "I already told you before Monday closed — if we deliver lower, we'll be looking for longs the next days"

**Intraday delivery pattern on non-reversal Mondays:**
- Market doesn't take a big liquidity pool in AM
- Engineers small liquidity during AM session (equal highs/lows)
- Delivery of the main move begins around 12–1 PM after that engineered liquidity is taken

### Weekly Opening Price

The **weekly opening price** is the price at which Sunday's electronic session first opens.

**Core rule (YouTube: First Week of March, March 2026):**
- Below weekly OP on Tuesday or Wednesday → **high probability longs** toward buy-side
- Above weekly OP on Tuesday or Wednesday → **high probability shorts** toward sell-side

This works in both trending AND choppy/range market conditions — it is the directional "compass" for the two highest-probability days of the week.

**Maximum probability condition:** Price is below the weekly OP + midnight OP + 8:30 OP simultaneously on Tuesday or Wednesday → highest confidence long.

**After Tuesday/Wednesday extremes are set:**
- Thursday and Friday become lower probability; the week's HOD and LOD are usually already printed
- Reversals on Thursday/Friday are suspect unless they follow a clean liquidity sweep with a 15M FVG reaction

## Speed and Velocity as Entry Quality Filter

"Speed and velocity" is MrWitness's primary language for confirming that a trade setup is working.

**Vocabulary in use:**
- **"Spooling"** = price accelerating smoothly away from a PDA toward the target; ideal behavior after an entry
- **"Losing velocity"** = price decelerating near a PDA instead of expanding; signals the move may stall or that conditions are choppy
- **"Speed and velocity needed"** = the move has started but hasn't confirmed yet — wait for acceleration
- **"Heavy"** = price sustained in one direction without meaningful retracement; wanted before declaring direction

**Entry quality checklist using speed/velocity:**
- After entering at a FVG or OB, price should immediately move toward the target — not retrace further into the zone
- If price returns slowly and keeps back-and-filling in the PDA → reduce size or cancel
- Fastest expansions ("low resistance runs") happen when price never revisits the PDA

**LRLR confirmation:**
- Candles stacking in one direction with small bodies and unfilled FVGs = LRLR (confirmed)
- Overlapping bodies, filled FVGs, back-and-forth = HRLR (stand aside or size down)

## First Presentation (9:30 FVG)

The "first presentation" is the **first fair value gap created after the 9:30 RTH open**.

**Properties:**
- An algorithmic price level: the market refers back to it repeatedly — same day, next day, and into future weeks
- Treated as a support/resistance reference throughout the session
- Bodies of candles should NOT close inside it if the directional idea is intact
- If price closes below first presentation → first confirmation of bearish conditions for the session

**Usage:**
- When MrWitness says "I want to see a closure below first presentation" → he needs that level to break before calling lower prices
- "First presentation hit" = one of his standard target completions for the day
- Volume imbalance immediately adjacent to the FVG can be included in the "first presentation" range
- Ambiguity: the 9:30 candle itself sometimes creates the FVG; other times it's the 9:40 candle. Use the first clear displacement FVG after RTH open.

## Liquidity Void

A liquidity void is a price range where **no delivery has occurred** — price moved through it entirely in one direction without any back-and-forth candle delivery inside it.

Distinct from a FVG: a FVG is a three-candle imbalance; a liquidity void is a larger empty zone with no trading on either side at all.

**Behavior:**
- The algorithm is in a "hurry" to return and reprice this range
- "Never short against a liquidity void" — do not fade price delivering into it
- The market will treat the void as a magnet until it is rebalanced

**When a void is rebalanced:**
- Rebalanced = daily candles have closed both above AND below it on separate days, meaning both buy-side and sell-side delivery has occurred inside the range
- Keep the void marked for at least a full week (5 trading days); only disregard it after it's been rebalanced
- A "back and forth, back and forth many times" inside the range = the algorithm efficiently delivering it

**Contrast with FVG:**
- FVG: can stay open as a BAG, or get filled as an IOFED — either is valid
- Liquidity void: always gets rebalanced eventually; do not expect it to stay open indefinitely

Source: YouTube — First Week of March (2026-03-07); Monday review showing price gravitating toward the void that had no prior candle delivery.

## Contract Rollover Protocol

Futures contracts roll quarterly. Using an expiring contract after volume shifts causes false "cracking correlation" that is not a real SMT signal.

**When to switch:**
1. Go to CME futures data (linked in Discord "notes and resources")
2. Compare volume between the front month and next contract
3. When the next contract (e.g., ESU for September) exceeds the front month (ESM for June) in volume → switch immediately
4. Trade the "fresher symbol" for better fills and cleaner price perception

**Why it matters:**
- The expiring contract (ESM) will start "cracking" as volume drains — looks like SMT divergence but is just stale data
- If you see ES and NQ cracking in an unexpected way near rollover → check contract dates before calling it SMT

> "You want to be trading the fresher contract. As the day passes you're going to start seeing the price on the ESM start cracking." — Stream: 0830 Mid Monday

## Live Trading Discipline (AXL)

Rules AXL follows and teaches by demonstrating live:

**Daily loss limit:**
- Accounts ≥ $10K: $1,000 maximum daily loss
- Accounts < $10K: $500 maximum daily loss
- Hit the limit → log out; come back tomorrow fresh

**Set and forget:**
- Only close at: (1) break-even, (2) partials at planned levels, (3) take profit
- Never manually close a trade outside these conditions — even if price action turns messy
- "My rule is break-even or take partials. I don't close my trade manually."

**No chasing expansions:**
- "If you see an expansion without a good opportunity, you're going to lose — simple."
- If price leaves without you → let it go; another setup will form

**Three requirements to win (AXL):**
1. Good psychology — impulse control, following rules under pressure
2. Rules — daily loss limit, set-and-forget, no chasing
3. A model that works — any backtested model
> "The model doesn't matter if you don't have psychology and rules."

**Recognizing bad conditions:**
- "Today is not conditions for trades" = AXL's signal to students to stay out
- Choppy price action (action-to-reaction, reaction-to-reaction) = accumulation; do not trade
- Pre-11 AM on Thursday (pre-NFP) = explicitly off-limits regardless of setup quality
