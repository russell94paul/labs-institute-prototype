## Class 1 Homework — Liquidity & Inefficiency

**Assigned by:** MrWitness, end of Vol 1 Class 1.

### Assignment

Back-test the following setup using **historical price action only**. No live trades. No demo trades. Analysis only.

**What to find:**

1. On the **1H and 15M** charts, identify **swing highs and swing lows** (three-candle patterns). These are your draw on liquidity (DOL).
2. Find a **displacement move** heading toward that DOL that created a **Fair Value Gap** on the **5M, 4M, 3M, 2M, or 1M** chart.
3. The FVG must be aligned with the DOL direction:
   - If DOL is a swing high above price (BSL) → find a **bullish FVG (BISI)** in the 5M–1M
   - If DOL is a swing low below price (SSL) → find a **bearish FVG (SIBI)**

**What to mark on the screenshot:**

- The swing high/swing low on 1H or 15M (your DOL)
- The FVG on 5M–1M (your entry signal)
- The **ideal entry level** — upper portion of the bullish FVG (Candle 3's area), one tick below
- The **stop loss** — below the three candles that form the gap (below Candle 1 of the FVG)
- The **target** — the swing high (BSL) on 1H/15M
- The **time of entry** (required — must be visible on the chart)
- Economic calendar for that day (add if possible)

**Deliverable:** Post 1–5 examples in the Discord homework channel. Review the pin messages first — MrWitness has posted examples of this homework there.

### What MrWitness Is Looking For

By seeing how you mark your chart, he will know how well you understand:
- Whether you are marking single price levels (correct) vs. zones (incorrect)
- Whether your FVG is on the correct timeframe (5M–1M entry, not 1H)
- Whether your entry is at the top-edge of the FVG (Candle 3 area), not the midpoint
- Whether the FVG and DOL are aligned (same direction)

> "At least one example, maximum five examples." — MrWitness, Vol 1 Class 1

## Class 2 Homework — Consolidation Model

**Assigned by:** MrWitness, end of Vol 1 Class 2.

### Assignment

Back-test the consolidation model. The goal is to demonstrate that you can identify a consolidation range, find the equilibrium, and frame a trade from a PDA near or below the EQ.

**Step-by-step:**

1. Find a **consolidation range** on the **1M or 5M** chart. Rules for a valid consolidation:
   - Bodies of candles define the range (not wicks)
   - The bodies are "encapsulated" — spending time in the range, back and forth
   - There is clear BSL above and SSL below the range

2. Mark the **50% (EQ)** of the consolidation range. This is the most important level.

3. Identify a **PDA at or below the EQ** (for a bullish setup):
   - Options: FVG, inversion FVG, order block, rejection block, volume imbalance, breaker
   - The entry does NOT have to be on the FVG — pick the PDA that is clearest on your chart
   - The PDA must be **at or below** the 50% (discount zone of the consolidation)

4. Mark your back-test trade:
   - **Entry:** at the PDA (below or at the EQ)
   - **Stop loss:** below the relevant swing that created the PDA (or below the PDA structure)
   - **Take profit 1:** low end of the consolidation (first target)
   - **Take profit 2 (optional):** previous day high/low (best case scenario — not required)

5. Add context labels to the chart:
   - Label the consolidation box
   - Mark the 50% line
   - Mark the kill zone (e.g., London Killzone: 2–5 AM)
   - Label your PDA type (e.g., "Inversion FVG" or "Order Block")
   - Label entry time (e.g., "4:14 AM entry")

**Deliverable:** Post in the homework channel.

### What MrWitness Is Looking For

> "You have to make it pretty. You have to like — you have to enjoy doing it." — Vol 1 Class 2

He wants to see:
- You can identify the consolidation range using **bodies** (not wicks)
- You marked the 50% (EQ) correctly
- Your entry PDA is **at or below** the EQ (in discount)
- The chart has time labels (kill zone context)
- You targeted the correct level — low end of the range first, then previous day high/low

### Example from MrWitness's Own Demonstration

MrWitness drew the following example in the class (using the same-day NQ chart):

1. Consolidation range formed from Sunday overnight to Monday pre-market (Asia session)
2. EQ marked at the 50% of the range (colored red for visibility)
3. During London (2–5 AM), the SSL (lows of the consolidation) was taken
4. SMT divergence at the lows (ES made a lower low; NQ did not)
5. Price closed back above the EQ (consolidation 50%)
6. Inversion FVG appeared at the EQ — the bearish FVG inverted to bullish support
7. Entry: 4:14 AM, at the inversion FVG, below the EQ
8. Target: previous day high (best case) / low end of the consolidation (conservative)

> "This will be your homework. You can find this in the Asia session, London, AM session, PM session, or lunch. It doesn't matter. Just find it."

## Practice Tips (From the Instructors)

**On back-testing:**
> "I don't want you to demo trade this. I don't want you to paper trade this. No clicking of the button. I just want you to find this ideal trade that you would take utilizing this concept and take a screenshot of it." — MrWitness, Class 1

## Readiness Check (Before Moving to Module 2)

Before starting Module 2, you should be able to:

- [ ] Mark a three-candle swing high and swing low on a 1H or 15M chart without hesitation
- [ ] Identify a valid FVG on a 5M or 1M chart (see the gap clearly between Candle 1's extreme and Candle 3's extreme)
- [ ] Distinguish a displacement FVG from a slow-moving range (no displacement = no valid FVG)
- [ ] Apply the IOFED rule: price returns to FVG, does not close below the CE → highest probability
- [ ] Identify a consolidation range using bodies only (not wicks)
- [ ] Mark the 50% (EQ) of a consolidation correctly
- [ ] Find a PDA (any type) at or below the EQ for a bullish setup

If any of these feel uncertain, do more back-test examples before advancing.
