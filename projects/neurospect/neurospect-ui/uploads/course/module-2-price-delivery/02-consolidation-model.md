# Module 2, Lesson 2: The Consolidation Model

> **Vol 1, Class 2 — MrWitness + AXL**

The consolidation model is the first and most complete trading model in this curriculum. Every concept you learned in Module 1 — liquidity, FVG, swing highs/lows, the DOL — comes together here. Master this one model and you can build an income from it alone.

> "You can harvest an income with this. You can use this for almost everything. Because this is the first pillar, the first column, the base of the price action delivery." — MrWitness, Class 2

---

## What the Model Captures

The consolidation model enters at the **beginning of an expansion**, from a PDA at or near the equilibrium of the range, after the manipulation leg has swept intra-range liquidity.

In plain terms: the market ranges (consolidation), sweeps the low (manipulation), bounces toward the middle of the range (EQ), and you enter on a PDA below the 50% — riding the expansion to the opposite side and beyond.

---

## Why This Works

The consolidation range is not neutral. It is actively engineering liquidity:
- **BSL above** the range: buy stops from traders who shorted the high
- **SSL below** the range: sell stops from traders who longed the low

When the market sweeps the SSL (takes the lows), it collects those sell stops. This provides the fuel (smart money accumulation at the lows) for the expansion higher. The 50% (EQ) is where the buy program begins activating — entries near or below the EQ are catching the beginning of the expansion, not chasing it.

> "You're going to see the displacement nature create a gap near the 50%. We close above the equilibrium. We came back to equilibrium. Respected it. And then we had our expansion." — MrWitness, Class 2

---

## Identifying the Consolidation Range

**Use bodies only — never wicks.**

> "In a Consolidation the high and low of the range are defined by the bodies of the candle, not the wick." — Class 2 Notes

Wicks extend outside the range (these are the liquidity sweeps and engineering). The bodies tell you where the range actually lives. Mark the top and bottom of the body range, then draw the 50% (EQ).

**Normal timing:** Asia session (8 PM – midnight Eastern), pre-market, or any overnight low-volatility window. The lower the volatility injection, the cleaner the consolidation.

> "Don't try this on a Sunday. There's no algorithmic principle on Sunday price action." — MrWitness, Class 2

**Signs you have a valid consolidation:**
- Candle bodies encapsulated in the range (back and forth, not trending)
- Visible equal highs or swing highs above (BSL) and equal lows or swing lows below (SSL)
- The range has been active long enough to engineer liquidity on both sides
- You can clearly identify the 50% (EQ)

---

## The Intra-Range Liquidity Sweep

Before the expansion, the market sweeps one side of the range. For a bullish setup, the SSL below is swept — a wick or brief candle dips below the range low and immediately rejects.

**What to look for:**
- A clear swing low (three-candle pattern, candle 2 lowest) at or below the range low
- The sweep candle rejects back above the range low immediately — it does not close below it
- The SSL is now "done" — the algorithm collected those stops and is ready to expand

**Optional confirmation:** SMT divergence at the sweep. If two indices (e.g., NQ and YM) take the low while ES fails to make the same lower low → smart money is accumulating. This is the highest-confidence version of the consolidation model.

> "This low was taken here during London. On ES, it was not taken. This is the first hint. This is what you should know as an SMT." — MrWitness, Class 2

---

## The Two Ways the Consolidation Can Resolve

**Path 1 — Direct (clean):**
After the sweep, price displaces immediately above the EQ, leaving an inversion FVG or FVG at the EQ. Price returns to the EQ once or twice, respecting it, then expands cleanly. This is LRLR (low resistance).

**Path 2 — With a BPR / choppy delivery:**
After the sweep, price reaches the EQ but has a balanced price range just below it — meaning price has already visited and filled the levels below the 50%. In this case, expect the market to bounce from the EQ but deliver more slowly and choppily. Still valid, but expect less clean candle behavior.

---

## Choosing Your PDA Entry

The PDA must be **at or below the 50%** (EQ) of the consolidation range. For bullish setups:

| PDA Type | How to Use |
|---------|-----------|
| **FVG** (most visual) | The displacement after the sweep creates a FVG; enter at or into Candle 3's area |
| **Inversion FVG** | A prior bearish FVG that price closed above; now bullish support — enter at its 50% |
| **Order Block** | Down-close candle before the displacement higher; enter at/above the body 50% |
| **Rejection Block** | Opening price of the sweep candle; expect a reaction after the MSS |
| **Volume Imbalance** | Treated like a FVG — enter at the 50% of the imbalance |

MrWitness in Class 2: "Pick the PDA that is most visual to you. Most of you will choose the FVG because it's the easiest to spot. That's fine."

## Worked Example: Live NQ (Class 2)

**Setup:**
- FOMC week (Monday, high-resistance conditions)
- Opening range gap ~41 handles (< 50 → choppy conditions expected)
- Consolidation: Asia range from Sunday night to Monday pre-market
- BSL: Wednesday's highs from prior week (pending business from 5 ticks away)
- SSL: Asia consolidation lows

**The sweep (London session, ~3:20 AM):**
- London took the Asia lows (swept the SSL)
- At that exact low: ES made a lower low; NQ did NOT → bullish SMT divergence
- Immediate displacement higher broke the student high (high that had taken the lows)
- 2022 model activated

**PDA and entry:**
- A prior bearish FVG was "inverted" — price closed above it
- This inversion FVG sat right at the consolidation EQ (killing two birds with one stone)
- Price visited the inversion FVG three times
- Entry at approximately 4:14 AM at the inversion FVG / below the EQ

**Exit:**
- Conservative target: low end of the consolidation
- Standard target: opposite side of the range (BSL above)
- Best case: previous day high → Wednesday's highs (the original pending business)

## AXL's Sniper Entry Version

AXL favors a more surgical version of the same model. Rather than entering on the FVG directly, he:
1. Goes to 15 seconds on the entry timeframe
2. Waits for a **change of structure (COS)** inside the PDA (equal lows swept → price closes above the internal high → that's the COS)
3. Puts the entry at the PDA (OB or FVG) at its 50%
4. Places the stop just below the three candles that formed the internal FVG on the 15s chart

This dramatically reduces the stop size. Class 3 example: "11 ticks in stop loss. 1 risk, 36 reward."

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | What MrWitness Says |
|---------|---------------------|
| Marking range with wicks | "Bodies tell the narrative. Wicks do the damage." |
| Entering before the sweep | The SSL is still outstanding — price may still go there |
| Entering above the EQ | "All entries are valid as long as they are below the 50%" |
| Trading Sunday Asia | "You should not be trying this on a Sunday" |
| Not having a clear PDA | "You always have to pick a PDA array" |
| Waiting for the best case | "The best case scenario is never necessary for you to make money" |
