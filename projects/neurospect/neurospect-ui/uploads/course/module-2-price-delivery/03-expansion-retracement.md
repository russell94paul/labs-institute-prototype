# Module 2, Lesson 3: Expansion & Retracement Model

> **Vol 1, Class 3 — MrWitness + AXL**

The expansion and retracement model (E&R) is the second delivery stage pair. After you've identified an expansion leg, you wait for the market to retrace into discount — specifically into a FVG or OB at or below the 50% — and enter there for a continuation to the original target.

This lesson also teaches you how to distinguish a **real retracement** from a **fake one**, which is one of the most common mistakes new traders make.

---

## Expansion: What It Is and How to Measure It

An expansion is a **strong, rapid, continuous move away from equilibrium**. It is not a slow grind — it is a displacement move.

> "The expansion is when prices pause or move rapidly away from equilibrium. It is a large movement, strong and continuous for a long time... it shows the will, the intention or desire of the market makers to reveal their interests — which is going to be the direction or bias." — MrWitness, Class 3

### How to Measure

1. Identify the **swing low** at the start of the expansion (the consolidation or retracement low that preceded the up move)
2. Identify the **swing high** that formed at the end of the expansion (the three-candle pattern capping the move)
3. Draw your Fibonacci or box from swing low to swing high
4. The **50%** line divides:
   - **Discount** (≤ 0.5): below — valid entry zone for longs
   - **Premium** (> 0.5): above — do not enter longs here

**Rule:** Do not re-anchor the expansion until a real retracement has been completed. If you see a new swing high form, that does not necessarily mean it's a new expansion — wait for the retracement to play out.

---

## What the Expansion Tells You

When a strong expansion leg forms, you now know:
- The bias direction (market maker intent)
- The 50% level (discount/premium boundary)
- The inefficiency left by the expansion — the FVG or OB that will attract price back to it

The expansion leg is your road map. Every subsequent entry is taken within the bounds of this road map (below 50%, into the inefficiency).

---

## Retracement: The Two Jobs

Once the expansion peaks (swing high forms), the retracement begins. For it to be a **real** retracement, it must complete both jobs:

**Job 1: Fill the inefficiency (FVG or OB)**
- Price must return to the FVG or OB left by the expansion
- These are found at or below the 50% of the expansion leg

**Job 2: Reach discount (≤ 50%)**
- Price must trade at or below the 50% of the expansion
- This activates the buy program for the next expansion

### Real vs. Fake Retracement

| Condition | Real Retracement | Fake Retracement |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Fills FVG/OB | ✓ | ✓ |
| Reaches discount (≤ 50%) | ✓ | ✗ |
| Result | Buy program activates → next expansion | More retracement coming; engineered sell-side |

> "So the retracement has these two jobs. Once the market is down here, there's something that the 50% is doing and is activating a buy program. This is the reason why the market is going to expand again. Because expansion is when prices move rapidly away from equilibrium — and this equilibrium is activating another expansion." — MrWitness, Class 3

**What fake retracements look like:**
The market fills the FVG near the 50% but turns back up without actually trading through the 50% line. This fools traders into thinking the expansion has resumed — but the real discount hasn't been reached yet. Expect the market to make another leg lower before the expansion resumes.

> "This is called a fake retracement. Fake retracement or engineered sell-side — the fake swing low may come all the way down here. And from here, you want to see that bullish delivery beginning." — Class 3

---

## Healthy vs. Choppy Retracement

**Healthy retracement (lower highs into discount):**
- Price makes a clean series of lower highs on its way down to the 50% + FVG
- This is engineered sell-side liquidity being built on the way down
- The result: a strong, LRLR-style expansion when the buy program activates
- "Beautiful for long" — MrWitness's exact words

**Choppy retracement (irregular, no clean lower highs):**
- Price moves sideways or makes equal highs instead of lower highs on the way down
- Less sell-side liquidity is engineered on the way down
- Result: slower, choppier expansion toward the target
- Still valid — but manage expectations and use a wider stop

> "If the market is delivering in this way, making lower highs, lower highs, lower highs — that is beautiful for long. Just beautiful. But if the market is taking longer and it's not making lower highs... there's going to be high resistance toward the buy side." — MrWitness, Class 3

## Entry: Where and How

**What to find:**
An OB (down-close candle before the up move) or FVG that is **at or below the 50%** of the expansion leg.

> From Class 3 notes: "Look for an OB / FVG that was left at or below the EQ of the Expansion."

**Entry rules:**
- Both OB and FVG at the same level = highest confluence ("OB + FVG 50% alignment strengthens setup" — Class 3 Notes)
- If a FVG is above the 50% and below the 50%: use only the portion **below** the 50%
- If only a partial portion of the FVG is below the 50%, wait for price to trade into that lower portion

**Candle body confirmation (algorithmic signature):**
The market gives you a signal that it is ready to resume: a candle body closes **at or above** the FVG boundary (for longs). This means the algorithm is respecting the gap.

> "Candle bodies respecting an FVG boundary (closing on/above it) = algorithmic signature for continuation." — Class 3 Notes

Once you see bodies respecting the FVG with closes above it, enter as the next candle opens. Stop loss: below the low of the candle that respected the FVG.

**Do not chase in premium:**
If price makes a new swing high before you entered, resist the impulse to buy. You are now in premium. Wait for the retracement to bring price back to discount.

> "We as smart money traders don't trade breakouts. We don't do it like this. Especially in premium. If the market breaks this high, there's a high chance of dropping you all the way back to this gap below 50%." — MrWitness, Class 3

---

## Stop Loss and Target

**Stop placement options (from Class 3 Notes):**
- Standard: below the relevant swing/OB
- Alternative: trail beneath the three candles that form the FVG (tightest option; valid if the entry is fresh and LRLR conditions hold)

**Target:**
- The original DOL — the swing high (BSL) that was the target before the retracement began
- Once the real retracement is complete and the buy program is active, the market is expected to reach that target cleanly

## Worked Examples (Class 3 — MrWitness live charts)

**Example 1 — February gap at discount:**
- Market expands higher, leaving a gap
- Swing high forms; retracement begins
- First FVG near 50% is not below the 50% → skip it
- Market continues lower to the "February gap" which is below the 50%
- Bodies close above the February gap: algorithmic signature for continuation
- MrWitness entered at this gap, stop at the swing low
- Second opportunity: another small FVG appeared at the 50% after the first tap
- "Taps it, then starts delivering higher and higher"

**Example 2 — OB and FVG aligned at 50%:**
- Market expands higher off a liquidity sweep
- Retraces; makes a higher high (MrWitness does not chase)
- Makes another higher high (still does not chase)
- Market finally delivers lower below the 50%
- An OB (down-close candle before the up move) is present at the same price as a FVG
- "Note how the 50% of the order block is aligned with the 50% of the fair value gap. Beautiful."
- Market touches it by one tick, then delivers higher
- Best entry: entering as bodies respected the FVG at the 50% — candle closes above the FVG boundary; enter as next candle opens; stop below the three candles of the FVG

## AXL's Trade (Class 3 — BPR + failure swing approach)

AXL demonstrated a different confirmation approach using a **Balanced Price Range** on the 30-minute chart:

1. 30-minute BPR identified (no wick — both buy and sell sides already delivered inside the range)
2. FVG formed after the BPR but did not take the equilibrium → two probable futures (volatility expected)
3. On 1-minute: market made equal lows → **failure swing** (market failed to take the liquidity target, turned at a rejection block) → COS (close above the high that preceded the failure)
4. Gantt box from swing low to swing high → waited for PDA on discount
5. Found OB on discount (the order block that failed to be taken = failure swing confirmation)
6. Entry at OB, stop below swing low
7. Result: 96 ticks stop loss, 1 risk, 10 reward

> "This trade was based around narrative. The other trade I'm going to show you is based on algorithmic price delivery. This is the difference: confirmations, external and internal liquidity taken." — AXL, Class 3

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|-----------|
| Entering at a FVG above the 50% | Wait for the portion of the FVG that is below the 50%, or find a different PDA |
| Chasing the expansion in premium after a fake retracement | "Sometimes you just want to not know what's going on and you just don't engage." |
| Confusing fake retracement for a real one | Check both jobs: FVG filled AND discount reached? Both boxes must be checked |
| Moving the expansion anchor after each higher high | Do not re-anchor until a real retracement is complete |
| Missing trades because you waited for the best FVG | "Sometimes you want to miss trades. I miss trades sometimes too." — MrWitness |

---

## Mindset Note (from Class 3 Notes)

> "Accept that: some trades will be missed. Not every FVG is tapped. The goal is quality setups, not constant participation."

> "Don't re-anchor expansions just because new highs form; only reset after a real retracement completes."
