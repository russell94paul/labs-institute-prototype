# Module 3, Lesson 1: Power of Three (AMD)

**Prerequisites:** [[concepts/course/module-2-price-delivery/04-reversals|Module 2, Lesson 4 — Reversals]]  
**Reference:** [[concepts/business-logic/ict-narratives|ICT Narratives KB]]

---

## Concept

Power of Three (AMD) is a **narrative framework** — not a picture-perfect pattern. It describes how the market delivers every daily candle (and every session candle):

| Phase | What Happens | Who's Doing It |
|---|---|---|
| **Accumulation** | Price moves below the opening price | Smart money buys from retail sellers |
| **Manipulation** | Price breaks below the open; retail sees "weakness" and shorts | SM accumulates at cheap prices |
| **Distribution** | Price rallies above the open and delivers to the target | SM sells to retail breakout buyers |

> "It is not necessarily that pattern [the textbook three-box picture], because what I teach my students to pay attention to in the charts are to the narrative, always narrative. When you have the right narrative, the patterns are going to be working." — Vol 2 Class 1

The **opening price** is the daily anchor. The wick below (on a bullish day) is the manipulation leg. The body above is distribution. Retail sees the drop below open as "weakness" and sells — smart money is buying from them.

---

## The Key Insight: Retail vs. Smart Money Psychology

Retail interpretation when price drops below the opening price:
- Stochastic says overbought → keep selling
- EMA clouds shifting → trend is down
- Below opening price = weakness → sell the breakdown

Smart money interpretation of the same move:
- Price is below the opening price → accumulate longs at a discount
- Retail selling = the sellers smart money needs to buy from

This is why the manipulation leg exists: the market needs retail to sell before smart money can accumulate. The low of the daily candle (on a bullish day) is the **evidence of this trap being completed**.

---

## Four Opening Prices — Four AMD Cycles Per Day

The AMD cycle does not happen once per day. It repeats **four times** — one per session open:

| Opening Price | Time (Eastern) | Session |
|---|---|---|
| Midnight Open | 00:00 | Daily anchor; most important for full-day bias |
| 8:30 Open | 08:30 | CME futures open |
| 9:30 Open | 09:30 | RTH open |
| 1:30 PM Open | 13:30 | PM session |

**The rule is simple:**
- **Bullish bias → look for longs below these opening prices**
- **Bearish bias → look for shorts above these opening prices**

Best longs form when price is below the midnight, 8:30, AND 9:30 opens simultaneously. Best shorts form when price is above all three simultaneously.

---

## Rules

1. **Narrative first, pattern second.** If the narrative is wrong, even a textbook-perfect AMD will fail.
2. **The manipulation leg is the wick.** The body above the open is distribution — that's where you exit, not enter.
3. **Below the open = buy zone on bullish days.** The further below all three AM opens, the higher the probability.
4. **The daily open is always the most important.** For intraday entries, the relevant opening price depends on what session you're trading.
5. **You have four chances per day** to apply this framework — one per session open. Missing one opportunity does not mean the day is over.

## Worked Example: Asia Session Trade (AXL — Vol 2 Class 1)

**Setup (1H–15M timeframe, Asia session):**
1. Market leaves an FVG on the 1H timeframe above price — bullish
2. Below the FVG: a 15M order block (the immediate reaction level)
3. Market leaves liquidity close to that 15M order block (equal lows / internal liquidity)
4. Market takes external liquidity AND internal liquidity at the 15M level

**Entry (1M–5M timeframe):**
- After both external and internal liquidity are swept
- Wait for a change of structure (close above internal high)
- Market retraces to equilibrium (50%) of the expansion move
- Enter at the FVG just below equilibrium
- Stop loss: above the swing high

**Result:** 1 risk : 28–29 reward. The trade completed before the London open.

AXL's step-by-step was: (1) 15M+ confluences, (2) 15M internal liquidity, (3) 1M change of structure, retracement to 50%, FVG entry.

> "When you don't have external or internal liquidity, you look for a PDR above equilibrium — something in OTE, not the first PDR above discount. Because that is the best entry based on the swing high." — Vol 2 Class 1 Transcript

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| Trading the pattern without the narrative | AMD without bias = random; the manipulation leg must make sense in context |
| Expecting the textbook three-box shape | Real AMD is messy; it's behavior, not geometry |
| Going short below the opening price on a bullish day | Below the open on a bullish day = accumulation zone, not a short setup |
| Taking a long above the opening price on a bullish day | Distribution phase — smart money is selling to you up here |
| Chasing after the manipulation leg completes | The distribution has already started; missing the manipulation leg means waiting for the next session's AMD |

## Homework

1. **Mark opening prices on your chart every day:** midnight open (orange), 8:30 (another color), 9:30, 1:30 PM.
2. **After each trading day**, identify the manipulation leg: which opening price was the anchor? When did price drop below (on a bullish day) and then rally above?
3. **Count the AMD cycles.** On most days, you'll see 2–4 clear AMD events — one per session. Mark them.
4. **Write down** for 10 consecutive days: was the low of the day formed below the midnight open? Or 8:30? Or 9:30? Track which opening price most often anchors the manipulation leg.
