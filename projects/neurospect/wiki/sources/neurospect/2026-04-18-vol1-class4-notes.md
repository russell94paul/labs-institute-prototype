Volume 1 Class 4 | Reversal 
 
Deﬁnition 
 
Reversal = Market moves in contrarian direction from the current trend. 
 
Happens when Market Makers (Men in Black) take a major pool of liquidity and 
shift intent to the opposite side. 
 
Real reversal must take out the origin of the current trend (the swing low/high 
where the trend began). 
 
Liquidity Levels for Reversals 
Reversals form at big pools of liquidity: 
 
Weekly highs/lows 
 
Daily highs/lows 
 
1H or session highs/lows 
The 3 Reversal Types 
1. Failure Swing (≈10%) 
o Market fails to reach the liquidity target. 
o Turns at a rejection block or week’s consequent encroachment (C.E.). 
o Looks like a retracement but isn’t (because it doesn’t tap an OB/FVG at 
50%) 
o Key: Take partials at rejection blocks since no one knows if it will fail or 
complete. 
GWP


 
2. Raid on Stops (≈80%) 
o Market runs the liquidity (e.g., highs), then rejects. 
o Classic “get in, get out” behavior → quick sweep then reversal. 
o Traps traders: 
 
Shorts selling resistance → get trapped when price spikes up. 
 
Longs buying breakout → get trapped on reversal. 
o Most common reversal pattern. 
3. Accumulation of Shorts/Longs (≈10%) 
o Not a consolidation → an accumulation at extremes near liquidity. 
o Retail thinks range = continuation, but SM is building positions for 
reversal. 
o DiƯerence: 
 
Consolidation = continuation (within a range, before target). 
 
Accumulation = reversal (at extremes, around liquidity). 
 
Key Rules 
 
Always target the swing where trend began → a true reversal must reach it. 
 
Failure swings are more likely under high resistance liquidity run conditions. 
 
Always take partials at rejection blocks— no one knows if price will fail or extend. 
 
Best probability reversals happen when layered liquidity pools (multiple 
highs/lows stacked).
