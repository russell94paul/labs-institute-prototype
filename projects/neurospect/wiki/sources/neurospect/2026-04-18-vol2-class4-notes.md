Volume 2 Class 4 | Daily Bias  
 
Core Concept 
 
Bias = Liquidity → IneƯiciency → Liquidity. 
 
Market is always going toward either liquidity or ineƯiciency – never both. 
 
Higher-timeframe (HTF) bias cuts through noise and tells you which side to favor. 
 
Step 1: Find HTF IneƯiciency 
 
Look for cleanest FVG/imbalance on 1H–4H–Daily (use higher TF if multiple 
overlaps). 
 
As long as price is inside that FVG, look for trades in direction of breaking out 
of it. 
 
Example: In a 4H bullish FVG → keep looking for longs until FVG is closed or 
violated. 
 
Step 2: Check Opening Prices 
 
Mark Midnight Open, 8:30, and 9:30 opens (color-code them). 
 
Bias ﬁlter: 
o If bullish → look for longs below these opens. 
o If bearish → look for shorts above these opens. 
GWP


 
Best setups: when price is below Midnight Open on bullish days (or above on 
bearish days). 
 
Execution 
 
Ideal longs: below open prices + near HTF support (FVG/OB). 
 
Ideal shorts: above open prices + near HTF resistance. 
 
Other Notes 
 Only use FVG for your Bias 
 
Key Reminders 
 
Stay with HTF narrative — ignore small bearish/bullish structures inside FVG if 
HTF bias points opposite. 
 
Each day resets → check HTF FVG status ﬁrst, then opening price position. 
 
If no clean HTF FVG → just target nearest external liquidity. 
 
Best trades form when: HTF bias + intraday position (below/above open) align. 
 
Quick Checklist 
 
Mark HTF range (1H–4H–Daily). 
 
Find main FVG or gap driving price. 
 
Decide bias: inside bullish gap → look for longs, etc. 
 
Mark Midnight, 8:30, 9:30 opens. 
 
Favor trades below opens (for longs) or above (for shorts). 
 
Target liquidity outside the range.
