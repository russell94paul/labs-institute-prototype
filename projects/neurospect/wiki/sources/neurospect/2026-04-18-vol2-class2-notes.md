Core idea (Session Power of Three) 
1. Opening prices + time windows drive the trade. 
Your model should key oƯ where price is relative to the relevant opening price 
during the KZ. 
2. Simple rule of thumb 
o Bullish day: During the KZ, if price is below the relevant opening price, 
look for longs back through/away from it. 
o Bearish day: During the KZ, if price is above the relevant opening price, 
look for shorts back through/away from it. 
3. Which opening price? 
o Asian & London: use the session opening price (Asian = 18:00; London = 
00:00; the “midnight open” becomes the main reference after London). 
o New York AM & PM: shift to Killzone opens because of the 08:30 CME 
open and the 09:30 RTH open. Track 08:30 Open and 9:30 Open 
speciﬁcally.

What to expect in each window 
Note: One of the Killzones (Asian, London, NY AM, NY PM) will usually set the day’s 
high or low. 
 
Asian (19:00–22:00): If bullish, prefer long setups below 18:00 open during the 
KZ; if bearish, shorts above it. “Asian can be quirky—worth its own study“ 
 
London (02:00–05:00): Same logic using the 00:00 open. 
 
NY AM (08:30–11:00/11:30): 
o 08:30 often creates/targets one side (can set the session’s high/low). 
o 09:30 may continue 08:30’s move or manipulate 08:30’s high/low before 
reversing. 
o Use 08:30 and 09:30 opens as magnets/decision lines. 
 
Lunch (≈11:30–13:30): Lower energy transition into the PM. 
 
NY PM (13:30–15:45/16:00): Use the 13:30 KZ open. PM frequently sets the ﬁnal 
high/low of the NY session. 
Terminology & small extras 
 
When he says “session,” or “session liquidity” he’s usually referring to the 
high/low formed inside the KZ windows (not the whole white box in the picture). 
 
The important / key highs and lows are formed in the Killzones. 
 
Opening Range Gap (ORG): the gap between yesterday’s RTH close (≈16:00) 
and today’s 09:30 RTH open. 
 
Highest Probability is when you search the long below all / multiply session 
opens or shorts above (See picture below, yellow and red are session opens) 
 
Targets: favor prior session highs/lows and obvious swing liquidity. Use 
standard tools (order blocks, fair-value gaps, consequent encroachment) to 
frame entries around the opening-price lines. 
Quick checklist 
1. Mark 18:00, 00:00, 08:30, 09:30, 13:30 opens. 
2. Know today’s bias (context/higher-timeframe). 
3. Trade only inside the active KZ. 
4. If bias is up and price is below the relevant open during the KZ → hunt longs; 
opposite for shorts. 
5. Aim for session/day liquidity (prior KZ highs/lows). 
That’s the essence: marry time (the KZs) with place (the right opening price), and 
let PO3 do the rest.
