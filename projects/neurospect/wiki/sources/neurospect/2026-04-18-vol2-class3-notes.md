Volume 2 Class 3 | Understanding Manipulation Using Deviations 
 
Timeframe: 
 15m-1h 
 
High/Low of Day 
 
Market “knows” the HOD/LOD from midnight open. 
 
Price action through the day is just delivery toward these pre-engineered levels. 
 
Exception: Manual intervention (rare). When it happens, expect a forced move in 
one direction, then a strong move in the opposite. 
 
Using Deviations 
 
Measure swing low → swing high of the last leg below opening price (for bullish 
days) or above opening price (for bearish days). 
 
Apply deviation levels (Fibonacci settings). 
 
Key Insight: 
o First deviation beyond a swing high/low = higher-probability target. 
o Don’t exit too early — aim for the ﬁrst deviation beyond the obvious 
liquidity pool. 
GWP


High-Probability Setup 
 
LOD/HOD often prints between −2 and −2.5 deviations. 
 
If −2 to −2.5 deviation aligns with a higher-timeframe draw on liquidity (e.g., 
Daily FVG, PD array), this becomes an ideal LOD/HOD. 
 
Deviations overlapping liquidity = High-Probability Draw on Liquidity (HPDL). 
 
Practical Application 
1. Identify midnight open as origin (0). 
2. Find ﬁnal manipulation leg before reversal. 
3. Plot deviations from swing low → swing high (or high → low if bearish). 
4. Targets: 
o First deviation past swing = target for partials. 
o −2 / −2.5 deviation (esp. inside HTF liquidity) = ideal ﬁnal target. 
5. Use 15m–1h swings for cleaner levels. 
 
Key Takeaways 
 
Trust your levels & targets. 
 
Take partials at ﬁrst deviation beyond liquidity, then hold runner toward −2 / −2.5. 
 
Overlap = Conﬂuence: If deviation & liquidity align, treat it as priority draw.
