The Architecture of Self-Improving Heterogeneous Reasoning Ensembles in Quantitative TradingQuantitative finance has historically evolved through distinct methodological epochs, transitioning from linear statistical arbitrage and factor-based models to complex, deeply layered neural network architectures. However, the pursuit of absolute return is continually hampered by the fundamental nature of financial markets: they are non-stationary, adversarial, and highly reflexive ecosystems. Traditional quantitative models, regardless of their complexity, generally rely on homogeneous predictive frameworks that ultimately suffer from epistemic closure. When a market regime shifts, a model trained purely on frequentist statistics or time-series momentum will inevitably experience degraded efficacy. To address this structural limitation, a paradigm shift is required, moving away from isolated predictive engines and toward what is conceptualized here as a "Self-Improving Heterogeneous Reasoning Ensemble" (SIHRE), theoretically branded as NeuroFusion-13.Unlike traditional ensemble methods that merely aggregate the final probability distributions of multiple identical or similar models, or standard Mixture of Experts (MoE) networks that route inputs to structurally homogeneous neural pathways, the SIHRE architecture proposes the fusion of completely orthogonal cognitive modalities at the intermediate representation—or reasoning—level. The system hypothesizes the integration of thirteen distinct intelligence typologies, including statistical, semantic, causal, adversarial, generative, self-supervised, evolutionary, and meta-learned paradigms. The foundation of this system rests on four core signals: semantic retrieval over structured trading methodologies, domain-specific language classification, gradient-boosted decision-tree machine learning, and a compounding temporal knowledge graph. This comprehensive report provides an exhaustive architectural review of the SIHRE framework, evaluating its theoretical viability, situating it within the current academic and industrial literature, defining the complex mathematical mechanisms required for true reasoning-level fusion, and outlining the profound theoretical limits of scaling heterogeneous multi-modal intelligence in a financial context.Lexical and Architectural Precedents in Quantitative LiteratureTo rigorously evaluate the SIHRE architecture, it is necessary to establish whether the nomenclature and underlying mechanics of a "Heterogeneous Reasoning Ensemble" currently exist within quantitative trading, systematic hedge fund research, or broader computer science literature. A thorough investigation of the current state-of-the-art reveals that while the exact phrase "Heterogeneous Reasoning Ensemble" is not a standardized term, its constituent theoretical components are currently the subject of intense academic and institutional focus.The Rise of Compound AI Systems in Institutional FinanceThe industry is currently undergoing a structural migration away from monolithic deep learning models toward what researchers broadly categorize as "Compound AI Systems". The concept of Compound AI Systems, formalized by researchers at UC Berkeley in 2024, posits that state-of-the-art performance in complex problem domains is no longer achieved simply by scaling the parameter count of a single dense model. Instead, optimal intelligence requires orchestrating multiple interacting computational components, including reasoning engines, short and long-term memory layers, semantic retrieval systems, and external computational tools. In the institutional finance sector, this is no longer purely theoretical. Platforms such as Compound AI and LinqAlpha have already begun deploying these multifaceted systems for fundamental equity research, market intelligence, and data room synthesis.The rationale for this shift is straightforward: financial data requires highly diverse processing mechanisms. A system tasked with evaluating a corporate merger must simultaneously parse unstructured SEC filings, calculate numerical revenue waterfalls, and understand the macroeconomic interest rate environment. Compound AI systems achieve this by routing sub-tasks to the specific model—whether proprietary, open-source, or rules-based—that is best equipped to handle that distinct data topology. Discussions at the ICLR 2025 Workshop on Advances in Financial AI explicitly highlighted this transition, noting that hedge funds are rapidly evolving from single AI models to integrated, multi-agent Compound AI architectures capable of simulating market behaviors and generating complex insights.The Evolution and Homogeneity of Mixture of Experts (MoE)To achieve multi-modal intelligence, many architectures rely on the Mixture of Experts (MoE) paradigm. Traditional MoE is a machine learning technique where multiple expert networks are used to divide a complex problem space into homogeneous regions. In natural language processing, sparsely-activated MoE architectures allow a model to drastically increase its parameter count without a proportional increase in computational load by routing input tokens to only a small subset of available experts.In the financial domain, this concept has recently been adapted for quantitative trading. For example, the "TradExpert" framework introduces a specialized MoE approach for stock movement prediction and trading simulation. TradExpert utilizes four distinct Large Language Models (LLMs), each explicitly specialized to analyze different sources of financial data, such as news sentiment, raw market data, alpha factors, and fundamental corporate data. A "General Expert LLM" is then deployed to synthesize these discrete insights and formulate a final trading decision, executing trades via a relaxed sorting algorithm. While TradExpert represents a significant leap toward multimodal market analysis, it remains primarily an ensemble of structurally homogeneous neural architectures. The experts are all large language models operating on text-based representations of data, executing late-stage semantic synthesis rather than acting as a truly heterogeneous cognitive engine processing fundamentally different mathematical realities.Neuro-Symbolic Artificial Intelligence: Bridging Logic and Pattern RecognitionThe fusion of traditional quantitative machine learning (which excels at statistical pattern recognition) with semantic reasoning (which excels at contextual understanding) naturally aligns with the tenets of Neuro-Symbolic AI (NSAI). NSAI is an emerging field that integrates the intuitive, pattern-matching capabilities of deep neural networks with the explicit, rule-based logic of symbolic systems, providing enhanced explainability, logical consistency, and rigorous risk management.In quantitative trading, recent breakthroughs have demonstrated the superiority of the neuro-symbolic approach. The "Program Sketch-based Tuning" (PST) framework, introduced in 2023, marked a critical milestone by embedding abstract human expert knowledge regarding market trends into deep reinforcement learning (DRL) models via a program synthesis paradigm. PST proved that neural-symbolic tuning could significantly improve the performance of state-of-the-art DRL strategies on order execution and stock trading tasks while adding minimal computational overhead.This foundational work was subsequently expanded into "Logic-Q," an advanced architecture that introduced a neuro-symbolic trend analysis mechanism (NeSy-TA). Logic-Q utilizes a plug-and-play market trend-aware program sketch to dynamically determine the current market regime and adjust the underlying reinforcement learning policy in a post-hoc manner across different data modalities. Furthermore, the academic exploration of "neuro-symbolic traders" has demonstrated that virtual agents using vision-language models can autonomously discover mathematical models of asset value, formulating them as stochastic differential equations that are then calibrated to real market data using gradient descent. These systems provide definitive proof that semantic understanding can be successfully mathematically constrained by symbolic and statistical logic.Multimodal Knowledge Graphs in Financial ForecastingThe final theoretical precedent for the SIHRE architecture lies in the use of persistent knowledge graphs for temporal market modeling. Financial markets are deeply interconnected ecosystems where dynamic knowledge graphs (DKGs) serve as rigorous mathematical structures for expressing complex topological relationships over time.Recent literature has highlighted frameworks such as FinDKG, which utilize fine-tuned LLMs to autonomously construct dynamic knowledge graphs from continuous streams of unstructured global financial news. These structures capture entities (e.g., central banks, specific assets, macroeconomic metrics) and map their causal or correlative relationships. Once the graph is constructed, attention-based Graph Neural Networks, such as the KGTransformer, are deployed to execute complex link predictions, evaluate trend dynamics, and inform thematic portfolio allocations, often outperforming traditional thematic exchange-traded funds (ETFs). Similarly, architectures like E-GraphNet model financial entities as asynchronous, multi-agent decision systems, integrating LLMs and Graph Neural Networks to enable cross-domain value mining in complex scenarios like enterprise mergers and acquisitions. Multimodal, knowledge-graph-aware frameworks provide crucial spatial-temporal grounding, vastly improving predictive validity by anchoring volatile statistical signals to a persistent ontological reality.The Foundational Topography of NeuroFusion-13To materialize the SIHRE concept, the architecture must cohesively bind four highly divergent primary modalities. The successful fusion of these signals requires complex mathematical translations that project discrete, structural, statistical, and semantic data into a shared continuous latent space, allowing the system to view the market through entirely different epistemological lenses simultaneously.Modality 1: Geometric Retrieval via Vector DatabaseThe first core signal relies on a Vector Database executing semantic and structural retrieval over a meticulously constructed trading methodology knowledge base. This base is specifically tuned to institutional order flow methodologies, such as Inner Circle Trader (ICT) and Smart Money Concepts, which focus on Fair Value Gaps (FVGs), order blocks, market structure shifts, and liquidity sweeps.Traditional quantitative models process price purely as a one-dimensional time series of open-high-low-close (OHLC) arrays or volume metrics. However, institutional order flow methodologies treat price action differently: as a two-dimensional geometric map of liquidity. Identifying a specific sequence of swing highs that leave structural imbalances requires understanding the spatial geometry of the chart, which is notoriously difficult for a standard Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) or decision tree to parse natively without heavy, rigid feature engineering.By embedding real-time market structures and candlestick formations into high-dimensional dense vectors, the SIHRE system can perform rapid cosine similarity searches against a massive database of historical, human-annotated chart patterns and theoretical rule sets. When the current market prints a specific price fractal, the Vector DB retrieves the closest historical matches and the theoretical logic associated with them. This modality provides a vital structural prior—a geometric interpretation of the market regime that contextualizes raw price data before any statistical processing occurs.Modality 2: Domain-Specific Language Classification (DSLM)The second signal is generated by a Domain-Specific Language Model (DSLM) fine-tuned explicitly on quantitative finance research, market microstructure phenomenology, and trading execution logic. Rather than outputting conversational, unstructured text—which is prone to hallucination and difficult to ingest programmatically—this DSLM is constrained to act as a structured classification engine.The DSLM ingests real-time news, earning call transcripts, central bank statements, and the contextual output of the Vector DB, and generates strict, schema-enforced JSON outputs. These outputs detail specific setup types, conditional confidence intervals, and binary flags for specific market criteria. For example, the output might dictate a bullish order block setup with an 87 percent confidence interval, explicitly verifying that specific conditions such as a liquidity sweep and price displacement have been met.This component acts as a vital bridge, translating the fuzzy, heuristic-based logic of human trading narratives and macroeconomic sentiment into deterministic, machine-readable feature sets. It provides the semantic reasoning overlay, capable of understanding nuance, sentiment distraction, and market narrative that frequently escapes the rigid bounds of purely statistical models.Modality 3: Frequentist Statistical GenerationThe third pillar introduces standard, highly optimized quantitative machine learning via gradient-boosted decision trees, specifically LightGBM or XGBoost. These models are trained on massive arrays of traditional quantitative market features, including high-frequency order book imbalance, statistical arbitrage spreads, volatility metrics, momentum oscillators, and cross-asset correlations.Gradient-boosted models excel at finding nonlinear, conditional relationships in massive tabular datasets and are uniquely robust in handling the heteroskedasticity and non-normality inherent to financial time series. This modality introduces strict, unyielding statistical intelligence, anchoring the broader SIHRE system in frequentist probability. It serves as an empirical counterweight to the DSLM; if the language model generates a highly confident semantic narrative about an impending asset rally based on a news catalyst, the LightGBM model audits that claim against historical statistical precedent. If the statistical edge does not exist, the narrative is suppressed, ensuring that semantic enthusiasm is always backed by historical mathematical validity.Modality 4: Temporal, Regime-Aware Knowledge GraphThe final core component is a dynamic knowledge graph that provides the system with persistent temporal and spatial memory. The majority of machine learning models deployed in finance are inherently static; they are trained on a historical epoch, deployed out-of-sample, and when their performance inevitably decays due to regime shifts, they must be taken offline and retrained.The SIHRE architecture escapes this limitation through a graph that continuously compounds with every executed trade, every backtest experiment, and every observed market anomaly. Within this graph, nodes represent diverse entities—specific assets, macroeconomic indicators, distinct trading strategies, and historical regimes—while the edges represent the causal, correlative, or temporal relationships between them.Unlike static models that suffer from catastrophic forgetting, the knowledge graph allows the architecture to track the degradation of alpha over time. If a specific Smart Money Concept, previously validated by the Vector DB and DSLM, ceases to be effective due to a shift in algorithmic market making behavior, the edge weights in the graph corresponding to that strategy will organically decay. This systematically reduces the influence of that specific pattern in future reasoning processes without requiring human intervention or a complete retraining of the neural weights. This transitions the artificial intelligence from a stateless predictive engine into an evolutionary organism that adapts to microstructure changes dynamically.Theoretical Scaling to a 13-Modality Cognitive MatrixWhile the core four signals provide a robust foundation of structural, semantic, statistical, and topological intelligence, the NeuroFusion-13 concept postulates the integration of nine additional modalities to achieve true epistemological supremacy. To fully map the market's hidden state, the SIHRE framework requires inputs from algorithms fundamentally specialized in alternative forms of logic.A causal inference engine is required to map intervention outcomes, distinguishing between spurious correlation (often identified by the LightGBM model) and actual market causation. An adversarial generative network (GAN) modality is necessary to constantly stress-test the portfolio, simulating synthetic "black swan" liquidity crises to measure the fragility of the current setup. A self-supervised representation learner would operate continuously on unlabelled tick-level limit order book data, extracting latent micro-structural features that are invisible to human-crafted rule sets. An evolutionary algorithm would serve as a meta-learner, constantly mutating the hyper-parameters of the fusion weights themselves, allowing the system's internal topology to evolve across decades of simulated market data.By scaling to 13 distinct modalities, the architecture guarantees that no single mathematical blind spot can compromise the portfolio. A market event that appears as random noise to a statistical model may be perfectly legible to a causal model, while a structural breakdown hidden from a language model would be immediately flagged by a topological graph network.Novel Topological Fusion at the Reasoning LevelThe defining characteristic, and the most significant theoretical hurdle, of the SIHRE framework is its rejection of simple late-stage ensemble methods. Traditional models utilize prediction-level integration, such as uniform averaging, majority voting, or basic linear regression over the final outputs of various models. This late-stage fusion prevents the models from sharing intermediate, nuanced insights; a momentum model cannot leverage a mean-reversion model's internal latent perception of a structural support level.To achieve true reasoning-level integration—where models communicate their uncertainty and context before generating a final output—the system must project the discrete intermediate states of the 13 modalities into a shared continuous latent space. Several advanced mathematical and architectural techniques must be orchestrated to achieve this deep integration.Reciprocal Rank Fusion for Non-Parametric ConsensusDuring the initial candidate generation phase, where the system must evaluate thousands of potential asset setups globally, it faces the problem of incommensurable scoring metrics. The Vector DB generates a cosine similarity score; the DSLM outputs a linguistic confidence probability; the LightGBM model outputs a log-loss based frequentist probability; and the Knowledge Graph outputs a topological proximity metric. These raw scores cannot be directly averaged or compared.Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF) provides a highly effective, score-agnostic mathematical technique for synthesizing these disparate outputs. Instead of attempting to normalize the raw scores, RRF ranks the results from each independent modality and merges them by combining their relative positions, giving each signal a proportional influence based on its rank rather than its absolute score. For any candidate asset $c$, the RRF score is calculated as:$$RRF(c) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{1}{\kappa + rank_i(c)}$$where $rank_i(c)$ represents the rank of the candidate in the $i$-th modality's list, $n$ is the total number of modalities (up to 13), and $\kappa$ is a smoothing constant, frequently set to 60 to mitigate the impact of extreme outliers and prevent highly volatile shifts in the ranking. This technique prevents a "winner-take-all" scenario where an overconfident, hallucinated prediction from the DSLM dominates the ensemble. It enforces a mathematically rigorous, democratic consensus among the disparate intelligence types before the deep computational reasoning phase begins. Furthermore, RRF can incorporate temporal decay functions, prioritizing more recent market data while maintaining hybrid relevance, which is critical in fast-moving exchange environments.Heterogeneous Mixture of Experts (HMoE) and Expert Choice RoutingTo manage the immense computational load of processing 13 distinct models simultaneously for every market tick, the system requires an advanced gating mechanism. Traditional homogeneous Mixture of Experts architectures utilize a top-$k$ routing function where tokens select experts based on a gating network's probability distribution. However, this often leads to severe load imbalances, where a few experts become over-specialized and overused (expert collapse), while others are under-trained. Furthermore, standard transformers exhibit extreme training instability when experts possess different network structures, which is why HMoE has historically been restricted to varying only the sizes of the experts, rather than their fundamental algorithms.To circumvent this and scale to 13 truly distinct algorithmic typologies, the SIHRE framework must employ an "Expert Choice Routing" mechanism. In this reversed paradigm, rather than tokens selecting experts, the experts calculate a compatibility score for all incoming data tokens and independently select the top-$k$ tokens they are most qualified to process, up to a fixed expert capacity limit.This routing mechanism is profoundly advantageous for a heterogeneous quantitative system. It allows a highly complex, systemic market scenario—such as an unexpected macroeconomic interest rate hike causing cross-asset contagion—to be routed to all 13 experts simultaneously for deep, multi-faceted processing. Conversely, a trivial market scenario—such as low-volatility sideways consolidation in a single obscure equity—might only be selected by the LightGBM and Vector DB experts. This radically reduces computational overhead, eliminates load imbalance, and ensures the optimal, dynamic allocation of cognitive resources based on the specific complexity of the market regime.Attention-Based Cross-Modality Signal SynthesisOnce the relevant experts have processed the data, their intermediate representations must be fused. This requires extracting the latent states before the final prediction layers: the aggregated embedding vector from the Vector DB, the final hidden state of the DSLM's `` token, the leaf-index embeddings from the LightGBM model, and the node embeddings generated via a GraphSAGE or KGTransformer network.These disparate embedding vectors are concatenated and fed into a multi-head cross-attention fusion module. Drawing inspiration from multimodal architectures that fuse image branches with time-series data using Convolutional Block Attention Modules (CBAM), this attention mechanism dynamically learns the optimal weighting of each modality based on the current market context. For instance, during a highly volatile macroeconomic news event, the attention heads may automatically suppress the LightGBM statistical embeddings—which rely on historical volatility metrics that are momentarily irrelevant—and drastically amplify the Knowledge Graph and DSLM embeddings, which are actively mapping the causal impact of the specific semantic news release. This heterogeneous multi-source fusion provides fine-grained control over the influence of macro signals on asset-level time segments, substantially improving both predictive accuracy and the economic interpretability of the model.Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning Chains and Hybrid ProtocolsThe final fusion technique involves the construction of explicit neuro-symbolic reasoning chains. This methodology is heavily informed by recent multi-agent collaboration frameworks like "ConSensus," which decomposes heterogeneous sensor inputs into specialized, modality-aware agents. The ConSensus architecture avoids costly, iterative LLM debates by employing a single-round hybrid fusion protocol that balances semantic aggregation with statistical consensus.In the SIHRE adaptation of this protocol, a Semantic Fusion Agent performs reasoning-level aggregation of the Vector DB and DSLM outputs, generating an initial narrative prediction. Simultaneously, a Statistical Fusion Agent anchors its decision to the frequentist outputs of the LightGBM and algorithmic models, providing a data-driven counter-perspective that mitigates the inherent semantic biases of language models. Finally, a Hybrid Fusion Agent acts in a coordinator role, jointly observing the latent states of both the semantic and statistical branches to produce the final, executed prediction.This creates a highly robust neuro-symbolic loop. The neural networks provide rapid, intuitive pattern recognition, while the symbolic logic ensures that the system's reasoning strictly adheres to established mathematical realities. Scaling heterogeneous multi-modal intelligence in a financial context introduces severe theoretical and practical limitations that must be rigorously managed.Data Leakage, Distraction Effects, and Lookahead BiasThe most pervasive theoretical risk when incorporating Large Language Models into quantitative backtesting is the introduction of lookahead bias via training data leakage. Any DSLM utilized in the SIHRE framework will have inevitably been pre-trained on vast, internet-scale corpuses of historical data, including news headlines, earnings reports, and historical market analyses up to its knowledge cutoff date.When backtesting the architecture on historical market data, the DSLM may exhibit an artificially inflated ability to predict stock movements. This occurs because the model implicitly recalls the outcome of the financial event through its pre-trained weights, rather than executing genuine forward-looking semantic reasoning. Studies indicate that when LLMs are presented with historical financial news, they often rely on memorized textual patterns corresponding to the subsequent price action, creating the dangerous illusion of predictive alpha.Furthermore, LLMs suffer from a "distraction effect," wherein their general pre-trained knowledge of large, famous companies interferes with the objective measurement of a specific text's sentiment, occasionally overpowering the lookahead bias itself. To mitigate this in the SIHRE architecture, rigorous de-biasing protocols must be implemented. This requires the systematic anonymization of all asset identifiers during the reasoning phase—forcing the DSLM to reason purely on abstract structural and statistical merits rather than company-specific memorization—as well as strict temporal partitioning between the pre-training cutoff and out-of-sample validation periods to prevent data leakage. Using membership inference attack (MIA) techniques to systematically test whether the LLM's forecasts arise from genuine reasoning or training data leakage is an essential auditing requirement for this architecture.Market Reflexivity and Endogenous DestabilizationFinancial markets are inherently reflexive ecosystems; the widespread deployment of a highly effective predictive model fundamentally alters the environment it is attempting to predict. If an architecture as comprehensive as SIHRE successfully captures structural alpha at scale, its own execution will begin to shape market microstructure, giving rise to self-referential feedback loops.Extensive literature on agent-based models of financial markets demonstrates that the interplay between fundamental pricing mechanisms and complex, trend-following algorithmic agents creates excess volatility, fat-tailed distributions, and spontaneous market crashes. Widespread adoption of predictive algorithms can destroy the very patterns they seek to exploit. Furthermore, the perception of trends by algorithms often leads to positive-feedback trading, which aggressively reinforces the existence of those trends rather than arbitraging them away, creating potentially destabilizing self-referential feedback loops induced by algorithmic herding.Within the SIHRE system itself, the persistent Knowledge Graph poses a profound internal reflexivity risk. If the system executes a massive trade that actively moves the market (e.g., executing a large order block in a low-liquidity environment), the resulting price movement will be ingested back into the Knowledge Graph as a successful, profitable prediction. This creates a dangerous positive feedback loop where the system inadvertently validates its own market impact as genuine exogenous alpha, leading to catastrophic overconfidence and the reinforcement of spurious causal relationships. To survive this, the system strictly requires the adversarial modality—one of the 13 proposed experts—to continuously audit the Knowledge Graph for self-referential bias, utilizing causal inference to distinguish between exogenous market movements and the endogenous noise generated by its own execution.Computational Friction and Latency ConstraintsFrom a practical engineering perspective, fusing 13 heterogeneous modalities at the reasoning level presents immense computational and latency challenges. High-Frequency Trading (HFT) and quantitative market-making rely on execution latencies measured in microseconds. The multi-step inference pipeline required by SIHRE—which involves high-dimensional vector similarity search, massive matrix multiplication in the DSLM, sequential tree-traversal in LightGBM, and continuous graph convolution in the Knowledge Graph—renders the architecture computationally unsuitable for ultra-low-latency environments.Furthermore, the non-differentiable nature of heterogeneous routing complicates the optimization of the system. In standard deep learning, gradients flow smoothly backwards through the network, allowing the model to learn efficiently. However, Expert Choice routing mechanisms, hard logical constraints within the neuro-symbolic chains, and the discrete boundaries of decision trees introduce non-differentiable steps that break end-to-end backpropagation. While sophisticated techniques like soft, differentiable approximations or reinforcement learning-based routing can bridge these gaps, they significantly increase the computational burden and exacerbate training instability. Therefore, the practical limit of the SIHRE architecture restricts its application to medium-to-low frequency systematic trading, macroeconomic alpha generation, and strategic portfolio rebalancing, where the holding duration of the trade heavily outweighs the computational latency incurred by the deep reasoning processes.SynthesisThe proposed "Self-Improving Heterogeneous Reasoning Ensemble" (SIHRE) represents a highly viable and theoretically profound advancement in quantitative financial modeling. The industry's nascent shift toward Compound AI Systems and multi-agent collaborative frameworks provides a strong empirical foundation for the integration of divergent cognitive tools. By utilizing advanced mathematical techniques such as Reciprocal Rank Fusion, Expert Choice Routing, and attention-based latent space integration, it is entirely possible to fuse Vector DB retrieval, semantic reasoning, frequentist probabilities, and topological causality at the reasoning level.This multi-modal synthesis actively resolves the inherent brittleness of homogenous quantitative models, seamlessly combining the fluid nuance of linguistic representation with the rigid empiricism of statistical learning and the spatial awareness of topological graphs. However, the successful realization of NeuroFusion-13 requires absolute governance over its inherent theoretical vulnerabilities. The system architecture must actively defend against the lookahead bias endemic to pre-trained language models, navigate the computational friction of non-differentiable routing, and implement internal causal auditing to prevent the Knowledge Graph from collapsing into a self-referential feedback loop. If these strict architectural limits are respected and engineered appropriately, the SIHRE framework possesses the potential to transition quantitative trading from a historical paradigm of isolated statistical prediction into a new era of holistic, multi-dimensional artificial reasoning.
