## Encoding DSLM outputs for LightGBM

The evidence supports the core premise that LLM outputs can sit beside traditional quant inputs. In non-financial tabular work, contextual LLM embeddings added to ensemble classifiers such as XGBoost and CatBoost often improve performance, and LLM-Boost explicitly shows that fusing LLM outputs with GBDTs can outperform either component alone across a broad range of sample sizes. In finance, ÔÇťText Meets NumbersÔÇŁ finds that prompt-based LLM extraction plus financial indicators improves earnings forecasting across Gradient Boosting Decision Trees and XGBoost; ECC Analyzer uses LLM-derived text and audio features from earnings calls for volatility prediction; and stock-movement work finds that LLM sentiment features can improve several downstream time-series models. ţłÇciteţłéturn23search7ţłéturn24view0ţłéturn5view1ţłéturn3view1ţłéturn29view4ţłü

The most defensible encoding pattern for a DSLM that has been trained on ICT/Smart Money concepts is a layered schema:

- **Atomic setup-state features.** Emit one column per condition or motif, such as `liquidity_sweep_present`, `FVG_present`, `market_structure_shift_present`, `OTE_zone_hit`, `session_alignment`, and `HTF_bias_agrees`. Also emit counts and ratios such as `entry_conditions_met = 4`, `entry_conditions_total = 6`, and `entry_conditions_frac = 0.667`. Low-dimensional, interpretable features like these are precisely what recent work on LLM-based feature generation argues for when the downstream learner is tabular and interpretability matters. ţłÇciteţłéturn24view3ţłéturn24view2ţłü

- **Calibrated scalar judgement features.** Emit bounded numeric scores such as `dslm_direction_score`, `setup_quality`, `displacement_zscore`, `liquidity_imbalance_score`, `semantic_pattern_score`, and `verbal_confidence_raw`. Then calibrate these scores ex post on the training window so that, for example, `0.72` means something like ÔÇťhistorically maps to a 58% conditional hit rate under this prompt/model/versionÔÇŁ. Adjacent LLM calibration work shows that generated confidence is often usable only after explicit calibration, and finance studies already compare confidence/probability-score representations against discrete labels in downstream prediction. ţłÇciteţłéturn8view2ţłéturn8view3ţłéturn29view4ţłü

- **Uncertainty and consistency features.** A single DSLM score is usually weaker than a small distribution of DSLM responses. In practice, useful columns are `self_consistency_vote_share`, `semantic_entropy`, `kernel_language_entropy`, `verifier_score`, `stepwise_confidence_robustness`, and `reasoning_length`. These are not yet standard finance features, but they are supported by the broader LLM uncertainty literature and are directly relevant if your DSLM is supposed to emit ÔÇťconfidenceÔÇŁ. ţłÇciteţłéturn8view1ţłéturn8view0ţłéturn6search0ţłéturn10view0ţłéturn10view2ţłü

- **Compressed semantic features.** If you want the model to capture nuance beyond a small fixed schema, do not feed raw rationale text to LightGBM. Instead, generate a finance-tuned sentence embedding for the rationale or matched motif, reduce it on the training set only, and pass a small number of components or similarity scores downstream. Financial studies already use embeddings, PCA, semantic similarity, and aligned multi-encoder spaces to turn text into predictive numeric inputs. ţłÇciteţłéturn25view1ţłéturn5view2ţłéturn8view5ţłéturn5view3ţłéturn29view0ţłü

A practical implication follows from the chain-of-thought literature: do not equate a fluent rationale with a faithful one. CoT traces can be plausible but unfaithful, and both causal-faithfulness and logical-verification work show that reasoning traces should be treated as noisy observables. In a production quant stack, the safer move is to use **derived metrics of reasoning quality** as features, while keeping the full rationale for audit and debugging rather than for direct ingestion by the booster. ţłÇciteţłéturn37view3ţłéturn10view1ţłéturn9search0ţłü

## State of the art in finance

The state of the art has moved from plain sentiment labels towards richer, multimodal and hybrid pipelines. Financial foundation models such as BloombergGPT, FinGPT, FinTral, and Open-FinLLMs all reflect the same trajectory: more finance-specific corpora, more structured and multimodal inputs, and more emphasis on downstream financial tasks rather than generic NLP only. Surveys of LLMs in finance and quantitative investment describe two dominant roles today: LLMs as predictors that extract or reason over information, and LLMs as agents that orchestrate broader workflows. They also note that practical deployment remains early and that hybrid integration with classical quant models is a major live research direction rather than a solved practice. ţłÇciteţłéturn22search0ţłéturn22search10ţłéturn22search5ţłéturn5view4ţłéturn1view3ţłéturn1view6ţłéturn1view7ţłü

Within systematic trading specifically, the cutting edge is not ÔÇťLLM aloneÔÇŁ. It is multimodal fusion, retrieval, and ensemble reuse of pretrained representations. The FinRL contest has explicitly focused on combining LLM-generated signals with reinforcement learning for trading. Open-FinLLMs reports strong performance across text, tables, time series, charts, and trading tasks. FinSrag introduces a financial time-series retriever that improves an LLM forecaster by retrieving informative historical segments rather than relying on surface-level similarity. FinAnchor aligns frozen representations from multiple encoders in a common space for financial prediction. Together, these papers point to the same architectural conclusion for SIHRE: the frontier is heterogeneous signal composition, not monolithic end-to-end prompting. ţłÇciteţłéturn1view4ţłéturn5view4ţłéturn25view2ţłéturn5view3ţłü

From a feature-engineering perspective, the useful finance-specific pattern is increasingly clear. The strongest published gains usually come from one of four approaches: extracting richer sentiment and topic signals than keyword methods, augmenting conventional indicators with embeddings or summaries, retrieving historical analogues to the current regime, or aligning several pretrained encoders and letting a smaller downstream model learn the final decision boundary. That is exactly the design space your DSLM-plus-LightGBM hybrid inhabits. ţłÇciteţłéturn5view1ţłéturn5view2ţłéturn25view1ţłéturn25view2ţłéturn5view3ţłü

## Confidence, coherence and similarity signals

There is not yet a large, mature finance literature that uses **chain coherence** or **confidence calibration** as first-class trading features in the same way volatility or momentum are used. But there is enough adjacent evidence to justify experimenting with them. Finance papers already use confidence-style outputs in tangible ways: stock-prediction work compares discrete sentiment labels with probability/confidence representations from transformer models; FinSrag uses generation logits as a proxy for the relevance of retrieved historical sequences; and recent disclosure research shows that LLM extraction combined with semantic similarity yields stronger return predictability than an NER baseline. These are not identical to your proposed ÔÇťreasoning confidenceÔÇŁ feature, but they are very close in spirit. ţłÇciteţłéturn29view4ţłéturn25view2ţłéturn29view0ţłü

The general LLM literature provides several good candidates for financial feature engineering. Self-consistency gives you a vote share across multiple reasoning traces rather than a single answer. Self-verification yields an interpretable validation score for candidate answers. Semantic entropy and kernel language entropy quantify uncertainty over meaning rather than wording, which is much closer to the relevant notion of uncertainty in market reasoning tasks. APRICOT and related calibration work show that confidence can be predicted and improved even when the interface is mostly textual. Stepwise confidence work adds a trajectory-level notion of robustness across the reasoning path. ţłÇciteţłéturn8view1ţłéturn10view0ţłéturn8view0ţłéturn6search0ţłéturn8view2ţłéturn10view2ţłü

For a quant implementation, the most promising features are therefore not ÔÇťthe full chainÔÇŁ. They are things like `consensus_across_samples`, `semantic_entropy`, `verifier_acceptance`, `faithfulness_proxy`, and `similarity_to_profitable_archetypes`. The supporting evidence for the last item is especially interesting: financial semantic-similarity work and ÔÇťFrom Text to AlphaÔÇŁ suggest that the way disclosures change relative to earlier economically meaningful patterns contains return-relevant information. In your SIHRE setting, a DSLM that scores similarity to dated, historically profitable ICT/SMC setups could plausibly be valuable, provided the pattern library itself is strictly point-in-time and the similarity model is also dated or evaluated out of sample. ţłÇciteţłéturn8view5ţłéturn29view0ţłéturn28search7ţłü

## Anti-lookahead and evaluation governance

This is the make-or-break issue. The strongest recent papers are unusually clear: pretrained LLMs can leak future information into historical financial analyses, and prompting alone does not fix the problem. Sarkar and Vafa show direct evidence of temporal lookahead bias and explicitly state that the remedy is to use models whose pretraining data contains only language produced prior to the analysis period. The ÔÇťMemorization ProblemÔÇŁ paper goes further, showing that LLMs memorise economic and financial data during covered periods, that instructions to respect historical boundaries fail, and that memorisation extends to embeddings. DatedGPT and Chronologically Consistent LLMs both arise as responses to this exact problem. ţłÇciteţłéturn32view0ţłéturn1view2ţłéturn37view2ţłéturn32view1ţłü

The design consequence is stark. If your DSLM was trained on corpora that include post-event commentary, realised outcomes, hindsight explanations, or future-labelled examples from the very periods you backtest, then any impressive in-sample result is fundamentally ambiguous: it may reflect reasoning, memorisation, or both. The literature argues that you cannot identify genuine forecasting skill inside the modelÔÇÖs knowledge window merely by telling the model to ÔÇťignore the futureÔÇŁ. ţłÇciteţłéturn1view2ţłéturn37view2ţłéturn32view0ţłü

The strongest control stack for a production SIHRE platform is therefore:

- **Use dated model weights whenever possible.** DatedGPT trains separate models with strict annual cut-offs, and ChronoBERT/ChronoGPT do the same at larger system level. ţłÇciteţłéturn37view2ţłéturn32view1ţłü  
- **Generate DSLM features in a true walk-forward loop.** For each evaluation date, freeze model weights, prompt version, retrieval index, and schema definitions as of that date. ţłÇciteţłéturn37view2ţłéturn32view1ţłéturn26view0ţłü  
- **Restrict retrieval and corpora to as-of sources only.** Time-aware pretraining and time-consistent retrieval are both now active research areas precisely because ex-post textual context contaminates apparent skill. ţłÇciteţłéturn37view2ţłéturn25view2ţłü  
- **Neutralise entity leakage when backtesting text-only tasks.** Glasserman and Lin find that anonymising company identifiers can outperform original headlines in-sample because general company knowledge can distract the model and distort sentiment measurement. ţłÇciteţłéturn37view0ţłü  
- **Use finance-specific leakage controls in downstream validation.** Current financial ML work continues to support purging, embargoing, and CPCV-style out-of-sample testing as defences against overlap leakage and backtest overfitting. ţłÇciteţłéturn34search0ţłéturn34search12ţłü  
- **Run contamination diagnostics.** The Lookahead Propensity test is a concrete new method for estimating whether prompt content appeared in pretraining and whether forecast accuracy is suspiciously correlated with that likelihood. ţłÇciteţłéturn37view1ţłü  

On prompt-versioned A/B testing, direct research in systematic trading is still thin, but the methodological pieces are there. Regression-testing work shows that model updates can flip the best prompt choice and argues that both prompt versions and model versions must be tracked. Empirical work on prompt evolution shows that prompt changes are often poorly documented and can silently change behaviour. Prompt-optimisation research, including DSPy, GRIPS, adversarial in-context learning, and bandit-based prompt-strategy selection, provides offline mechanisms for prompt search rather than ad hoc trial-and-error. In finance specifically, Zhuokai Chen reports that prompt variations changed the efficacy of an LLM trading filter, while identical prompts yielded consistent outputs. ţłÇciteţłéturn26view0ţłéturn26view1ţłéturn26view2ţłéturn26view3ţłéturn27search0ţłéturn27search2ţłéturn27search3ţłéturn30view0ţłü

For SIHRE, that means prompt A/B testing should be treated as **model governance**, not prompt tinkering. The right unit of comparison is not ÔÇťwhich prompt sounds betterÔÇŁ, but ÔÇťwhich prompt version generates features that most improve downstream out-of-sample information coefficient, calibration, turnover-adjusted PnL, and stability across purged date blocksÔÇŁ. That recommendation is an inference from the governance and prompt-optimisation literature, but it is the one most consistent with the evidence reviewed above. ţłÇciteţłéturn26view0ţłéturn26view2ţłéturn26view3ţłéturn27search0ţłü

## How to make the hybrid stronger

The most promising next step is a **residual hybrid**: let the DSLM produce a prior score or prior log-odds for the trade thesis, then let LightGBM learn the residual conditional on conventional quant inputs such as realised volatility, spread, order-book imbalance, session effects, and execution context. That architecture is directly analogous to LLM-Boost, where tree models improve on transformer priors by learning what the pretrained model misses. In a trading context, it gives you a clean decomposition: the DSLM estimates narrative/setup quality, while the booster adjusts for market mechanics and regime-specific failure modes. ţłÇciteţłéturn24view0ţłü

A second strong direction is **uncertainty-gated routing**. Instead of always trusting the DSLM, route the observation between a quant-only branch, a DSLM-enhanced branch, and a no-trade branch based on semantic entropy, self-consistency, self-verification, or stepwise confidence robustness. This is an inference from the uncertainty literature rather than an established fund standard, but it is well supported by the fact that modern LLM uncertainty measures outperform naive token confidence and that self-verification scores can be meaningful. ţłÇciteţłéturn8view0ţłéturn6search0ţłéturn10view0ţłéturn10view2ţłü

A third direction is **retrieval-to-feature memory**, where the DSLM does not merely ÔÇťreason from scratchÔÇŁ about the current setup. Instead, it retrieves dated historical analogues and emits features such as `similarity_to_profitable_pattern`, `similarity_to_failed_pattern`, `top_k_outcome_dispersion`, or `retrieved_pattern_consensus`. FinSrag shows that better historical retrieval can improve financial forecasting beyond surface pattern matching, while disclosure work using semantic similarity shows that semantically grounded comparisons can be return-predictive. ţłÇciteţłéturn25view2ţłéturn29view0ţłü

A fourth direction is **multi-view signal generation** rather than single-view prompting. The same market state can be described as an ICT setup, a market-microstructure state, a macro-event state, or a cross-asset regime. Open-FinLLMs, FinAnchor, and multimodal stock-prediction papers all suggest that gains often come from combining multiple representational views rather than pushing one model harder. In SIHRE terms, that argues for several DSLM sub-prompts or sub-models that each specialise in a different ontology, with the downstream booster learning when they are complementary. ţłÇciteţłéturn5view4ţłéturn5view3ţłéturn25view4ţłéturn5view5ţłü

A fifth direction is **time-partitioned DSLMs**. If temporal leakage is a real risk and market structure changes over time, then a family of regime- or date-specific DSLMs may be better than one universal model. DatedGPT and ChronoBERT/ChronoGPT show the feasibility of building temporally bounded language models, and Two SigmaÔÇÖs public comments on point-in-time models point in the same direction. For a live SIHRE system, a regime-aware selector over dated DSLM variants could be more robust than a single timeless model. ţłÇciteţłéturn37view2ţłéturn32view1ţłéturn18search0ţłü

## Open questions and limitations

A few important gaps remain. First, the direct literature on using **generative reasoning metrics themselves** as alpha features is still small; most of the strongest evidence comes from adjacent uncertainty, calibration, and semantic-similarity research rather than from mature systematic-trading deployments. Second, many finance papers in this area are still preprints, are tested on limited universes or short windows, and often understate costs, latency, and contamination risk. Third, public disclosures from major quant funds are sparse by design, so any claim beyond what is explicitly stated in official material quickly becomes speculative. ţłÇciteţłéturn1view3ţłéturn1view7ţłéturn12search7ţłéturn28search16ţłéturn35search0ţłéturn19search0ţłéturn21news41ţłü

The highest-confidence conclusion, therefore, is not that DSLMs will replace quant models. It is that a **well-governed DSLM can be a high-value structured signal generator** inside a broader quant stack, especially when its outputs are typed, calibrated, uncertainty-aware, semantically grounded, and evaluated under strict point-in-time controls.
