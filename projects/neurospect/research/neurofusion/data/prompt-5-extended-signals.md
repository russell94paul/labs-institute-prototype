### Conformal prediction

**State of the art.** Conformal prediction is one of the cleanest current tools for uncertainty quantification because it wraps arbitrary predictive models and gives finite-sample coverage guarantees with minimal assumptions, classically under exchangeability. The modern frontier is no longer plain split conformal on i.i.d. data; it is **time-series conformal prediction** under serial dependence, online recalibration, and adaptive quantile estimation. **Application to quant trading.** Finance-specific work is emerging but still relatively thin. ### Multi-agent adversarial verification

## Synthetic worlds, research efficiency and collaborative learning

## What would make SIHRE genuinely novel

The clearest dividing line between an incremental build and a genuinely new architecture is whether the modules are merely **stacked** or are **scientifically coupled**. The literature strongly suggests that each ingredient works best when its assumptions are explicit and its failure modes are monitored: causal systems need refutation and drift handling; conformal systems need sequential recalibration; debate systems need safeguards against persuasion failure; synthetic generators need controllability and downstream validation; and drift-aware ensembles need expert creation, deletion, and routing. A novel SIHRE would therefore encode these as *cross-module contracts*, not optional extras. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn6search6Ņłéturn37view3Ņłéturn12search1Ņłéturn38view2Ņłéturn34search8Ņłéturn40view3Ņłü

Concretely, the architecture looks most novel if it has a **shared event ontology** and evidence substrate. Your retrieval system, knowledge graph, causal layer, self-supervised regime layer, and LLM strategy generator should all speak in the same typed objects: event primitives, regime descriptors, instrument context, time horizon, uncertainty state, and execution assumptions. That would let the causal module ask whether an event is a plausible parent, the synthetic generator condition on the same event type, the debate agents stress-test the same object, and the meta-orchestrator learn signal value conditional on exactly defined states. Without that common language, the stack risks becoming a heterogeneous bag of unrelated scores. The open literature shows partial versions of this ideaŌĆöheterogeneous reasoners under a meta-reasoner in GILA, and dynamically combined alpha factors in AlphaForgeŌĆöbut not this full finance-native closure. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn33search11Ņłéturn33search8Ņłéturn40view2Ņłü

A second novelty criterion is that **causality and conformal prediction should act as governors, not just predictors**. Existing financial architectures often use dynamic weights or multi-agent selection, but they usually do not impose a rule like: ŌĆ£do not execute if causal stability collapsed across recent regimes,ŌĆØ or ŌĆ£scale position only if empirical conformal coverage on this signal family remains within tolerance.ŌĆØ That would be a meaningful departure from current alpha-mining systems, which are mostly performance-first. A third novelty criterion is **targeted failure generation**.
