# Phase 13: Go-to-Market & User Acquisition

Define and execute the strategy for acquiring the first 100 paying users. The best product doesn't matter if nobody finds it.

## Why This Phase Exists

Both plans describe the product in detail but neither explains how to get the first 100 paying users. No channel strategy, no launch playbook, no community seeding plan. This phase runs parallel to Phases 4-6 so GTM readiness aligns with product readiness.

## Goals

1. **Channel strategy** — Identify and validate the top 3 acquisition channels for ICT traders (Discord communities, YouTube, Twitter/X, trading forums, Reddit, instructor referrals)
2. **Community seeding** — Build a waitlist and engaged community before launch, not after
3. **Launch playbook** — Step-by-step plan for private beta invite → public beta → paid launch
4. **First 100 users plan** — Specific, measurable tactics to acquire the first 100 paying subscribers
5. **Content marketing strategy** — What content NeuroSpect publishes to attract organic traffic (blog, YouTube, social)
6. **Instructor-as-channel** — Strategy for leveraging instructor's existing audience as primary acquisition channel

## Acquisition Channel Analysis

| Channel | Pros | Cons | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instructor referral | Highest trust, warm audience, low CAC | Single point of failure, instructor dependency | P0 |
| ICT Discord/Telegram groups | High intent, concentrated audience | Saturated with noise, may ban self-promotion | P1 |
| Twitter/X trading community | Large reach, viral potential, ICT has massive presence | Noisy, hard to convert, requires consistent posting | P1 |
| YouTube content | Evergreen, SEO benefits, demonstrates product value | Slow to build, production effort | P2 |
| Reddit (r/Daytrading, r/FuturesTrading) | Organic discovery, long-form discussion | Anti-promotion culture, requires genuine value-add | P2 |
| SEO / blog | Compounding organic traffic | Slow (6-12 months to rank), competitive keywords | P3 |

## Launch Sequence

1. **Private Alpha (Phase 5)** — 10-20 hand-picked users (instructor's top students). Goal: product feedback, not revenue.
2. **Closed Beta (Phase 5-6)** — 50-100 users via waitlist. Goal: validate retention, pricing, and coaching quality.
3. **Open Beta (Phase 6)** — 200+ users. Goal: stress test infrastructure, validate acquisition channels.
4. **Paid Launch (Phase 6)** — Stripe tiers live. Goal: first revenue within 7 days.

## Exit Criteria

1. Waitlist has 200+ signups before beta launch
2. At least 2 acquisition channels validated (measurable signups)
3. Launch playbook documented and reviewed
4. Conversion funnel instrumented in analytics
5. Pricing validated with at least 20 beta users
