# Competitor Roster Presentation Patterns

## How UK agencies present their rosters

| Agency | Roster Style | Profile Content | Booking CTA |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Primary Talent | A-Z directory, linked names | Long-form credentials + upcoming dates | Direct contact on profile |
| Analog Agency | Alphabetical list | Short narrative bio | Email booking link |
| RFYN | A-Z with photos | Narrative + quantifiable proof (streams, chart positions) | Booking inquiry link |
| Coda Agency | Grid with photos | Genre tags + short bio | Contact form |
| Resident Advisor | Profile cards | Event timeline, stats, regions, venues | External links |

## Key Pattern: Story + Stats

The most persuasive profiles combine:
1. **Narrative** — who they are, where they came from
2. **Credentials** — specific numbers (streams, chart positions, years active)
3. **Social proof** — who supports them, what labels they've released on
4. **Activity** — upcoming/recent events showing they're active

Example (RFYN): "With over a million Spotify streams and a debut single that hit #22 on the UK charts, Cristian MJC is a UK-based DJ with 18 years of experience"

## Apply to Labs

Labs should replicate: concise bio + hard metrics + career timeline + embedded audio + booking CTA.
